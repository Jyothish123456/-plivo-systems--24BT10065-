# NOTES — Design Summary

I used dual-layer XOR-based forward error correction (FEC) instead of retransmission, since a NACK round-trip through the relay takes 40–160ms and usually misses the 20ms frame deadline. The sender XORs every pair of payloads (K=2) and every group of four (K=4), sending one parity packet per pair and one per quad — the K=2 parity recovers most single losses quickly, while the K=4 parity covers cases where the K=2 parity packet itself gets dropped. Total overhead is fixed at 1.81× with zero feedback traffic, so there's no risk of blowing the 2.0× cap. On the receiver side, every data packet is forwarded to the player immediately; FEC recovery kicks in only when a loss is detected and at least one parity packet is available for that group. I tried NACK-based retransmission in an earlier iteration but it pushed overhead past 2.0× without meaningfully reducing misses, so I dropped it. Grade at **delay_ms = 100** — it passed Profile B (5% loss, 20–80ms jitter) across seeds 1, 42, 99, and 7, worst case 0.60% misses. The design breaks under burst losses spanning 4+ consecutive frames (overwhelms both FEC layers) or if relay delay consistently exceeds the playout budget.
